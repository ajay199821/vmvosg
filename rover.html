Scheme of AdvancementRover

Motto: "Service"

Rover Promise

“On my honour, I promise that I will do my best To do my duty to God* and my country, To help other people and To obey the Scout Law’

* The word ‘Dharma’ may be substituted for the word “God” if so desired.

Cub Law

(i) A Rover is Trustworthy.
(ii) A Rover is loyal.
(iii) A Rover is a friend to all and a brother to every other Rover.
(iv) A Rover is courteous.
(v) A Rover is a friend to animals and loves nature.
(vi) A Rover is disciplined and helps protect public property.
(vii) A Rover is courageous.
(viii) A Rover is thrifty.
(ix) A Rover is pure in thought, word and deed.



A boy whether previously a Scout or not and who is a citizen of India and who has completed 15 years of age may be enlisted as a Rover ASPIRANT, A person who has completed 25 years of age is not eligible to be a Rover, but he can continue as a service Rover up to the age of 35 years.

*Wherever it is necessary, extension be allowed for three years with special permission of the State Chief Commissioner. He will be eligible for Rashtrapati Rover Award provided the special permission is granted before completion of 25 years of age and the same shall be reported to the National Headquarters.

A Rover Aspirant can be invested as a Rover only after completion of fifteen years of age and should have worked for three months for Pravesh Test.

Pravesh Rover

A Rover Aspirant will work at least for three months to complete Pravesh test before investiture.

Nipun Rover

A Pravesh Rover will work at least for six months to qualify for Nipun Badge

Rajya Puraskar Rover

A Nipun Rover will work at least for nine months to qualify for Rajya Puraskar Rover Badge.

Rashtrapati Rover Award

A Rajya Puraskar Rover will work at least for one year to qualify for Rashtrapati Rover Award.
